Other QC Metrics

  • Read Quality - Filter on reads with over 30 in Q30 scores
  • Datasets with low Q30 scores in barcode read have poor cell assignment
    • poor Q30 scores in the RNA read will not be able to be aligned
    • Typical values 60-70% throughout, with highest in the barcode read
  • Alignment QC - 40% or higher exonic reads is good
  • Direct calculation ? - the % of reads that are in the dataset after count matrix is made. Can estimate the cell input (hemacytometer counts, flow counts) If we get around the same number of cells in a count matrix, amazing.

QC metrics must be considered together, not in isolation. EG high mt counts may suggest involvement in respiration. Cells/barcodes with low counts may be quiescent cells, high counts may be physically large cells.

Normalization

The wetlab process has a lot of room for error. Cell digestion, isolation, sorting, capture/bead, RT, sequencing.
Count depth can vary greatly simply due to these processes, even when processed the same. Therefore identical cells could potentially look very different.
Normalization is required to address this

Linear

a simple way is to scale the gene expression per cell by the total library size
CPM normalization. or scaling by the median count depth per cell
the assumption here is that all cells initially had equal RNA and the only difference in RELATIVE amounts is due to sampling error.
Downsampling has the same assumption. It randomly samples reads/counts so that all cells have a certain # of counts or less. (deletes data)
These approaches aren't great for mixed populations. Maybe they are good for very uniform populations
this pooling method (scran) does a linear regression of genes after pooling all the cells, to estimate a size factor. and available here?
Scran outperforms other normalization methods one example in batch correction and DE analysis

Normalization with bulk methods was used at first but zero-inflated sc Data required new approaches
one example and a second one https://doi.org/10.1038/nmeth.4292
